Braihadisvara Temple

In 1010 AD, Raja Raja built the Brihadeswara Templein Thanjavur dedicated to Lord Siva. the temple and the capital acted as a center of both religious and ecnomic activity. it is also known as periya kovil, Raja Rajeswara Temple and Rajarajeswaram. it is one of the largest temple in india and is an example of Dravidan architecture durinng the chola period. The temple turned 1000 years old in 2010. The Temple is part of the UNESCO world heritage site known as the Great living Chola Temple, with the other two being the Gangaikonda Cholapuram and Airavatesvara temple.
